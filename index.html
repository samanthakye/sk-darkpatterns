Misinformation & Dark Patterns

3 Examples for Homework

Example 1: Forced Continuity

Type: Dark Pattern

Who benefits: The platform (they make money from subscriptions)

Description: Some apps ask for your credit card for a “free trial” but then charge you automatically without a clear reminder.

Example 2: Confirmshaming

Type: Dark Pattern

Who benefits: The company (more people sign up for emails)

Description: A pop-up says “Do you want our deals?” The “No” button says something like “No, I don’t like saving money,” making you feel bad for clicking it.

Example 3: Misleading Headline

Type: Misinformation

Who benefits: The publisher (they get more clicks and ads)

Description: An article headline makes a big claim that isn’t true, but people click because it sounds exciting.
